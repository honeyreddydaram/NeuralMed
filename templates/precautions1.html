Emergency Reference

First Aid Guide

Immediate care steps for common medical emergencies. Always call emergency services for life-threatening situations.

🫀 ABCs of First Aid

For unconscious persons — check Airway, Breathing, Circulation.

  • Airway: Open the airway if not breathing.
  • Breathing: Provide rescue breathing if needed.
  • Circulation: Do chest compressions to circulate blood.

🫀 Cardiac Arrest / CPR

  1. Have someone call 911 immediately.
  2. Start chest compressions — push hard and fast in the centre of the chest.
  3. Let the chest recoil between compressions.
  4. Use an AED if available. Do not delay compressions to find one.
  5. Continue until trained help arrives.

🩸 Bleeding

  1. Wear gloves if available.
  2. Rinse wound with water.
  3. Cover with gauze or clean cloth and apply direct pressure.
  4. Elevate the bleeding body part above heart level.
  5. Do not remove soaked cloth — add more layers on top.
  6. Get medical help if wound is deep, won't stop, or involves arterial bleeding.

🤧 Choking — Heimlich Maneuver

  1. Ask "Are you choking?" — if coughing or talking, do not intervene.
  2. Stand behind the person, lean them slightly forward.
  3. Place clenched fist between belly button and rib cage.
  4. Grab fist with other hand and pull sharply backward and upward.
  5. Repeat 5 times until object is dislodged.

Infant: 5 back blows face-down, then 5 chest thrusts face-up.

🔥 Burns

  1. Stop the burning: remove from heat, cool with running water.
  2. Flush burn with cool water for several minutes (no ice).
  3. Apply light gauze bandage; aloe vera for minor burns.
  4. Take ibuprofen or acetaminophen for pain.
  5. Do not break blisters.

Call 911 for major burns (face, hands, joints, or >3 inches wide).

🦴 Fractures & Broken Bones

  1. Do not try to straighten the bone.
  2. Immobilize with a splint and padding.
  3. Elevate the limb.
  4. Apply cold pack (wrapped in cloth — no direct ice).
  5. Give ibuprofen or naproxen for pain.

Call 911 for spinal, hip, pelvis injuries or compound fractures.

🦵 Sprains

  1. Stop activity and rest the injured joint.
  2. Apply cold pack for 20 min (wrapped in cloth).
  3. Elevate the injured area if safe.
  4. Use NSAIDs for pain relief.

Seek care if severe pain, can't bear weight, or numbness is present.

👃 Nosebleeds

  1. Lean slightly forward (not back).
  2. Pinch nose just below the bridge.
  3. Hold for 5 min; check; continue for up to 15 min total.
  4. Apply cold pack to bridge of nose.

Call 911 if bleeding won't stop after 15 minutes or follows a head injury.

🥶 Frostbite

  1. Get out of the cold immediately.
  2. Warm affected area in 98–105°F water for 20–30 minutes.
  3. Do not rub the affected area.
  4. Do not use dry heat sources (heating pads, fireplaces).
  5. Loosely bandage and seek medical attention promptly.

🐝 Bee Stings

  1. Remove stinger by scraping (not squeezing) with a credit card.
  2. Wash area with soap and water.
  3. Apply cold pack (not directly on skin).
  4. Antihistamine (Benadryl) to reduce swelling and itching.

Call 911 immediately for signs of anaphylaxis: hives, swelling, chest pain, trouble breathing.

🩹 Blisters

Small blisters: leave alone, cover to prevent rubbing.

Large or painful blisters:

  1. Wash hands thoroughly.
  2. Sterilize a needle with alcohol; make small hole at edge.
  3. Gently drain fluid.
  4. Apply antibiotic ointment and bandage.

Do not pop blisters if you have a compromised immune system.

🧰 First Aid Kit Essentials

  • Adhesive bandages (multiple sizes)
  • Gauze pads and compress dressings
  • Adhesive cloth tape & roll gauze
  • Disposable gloves & antiseptic wipes
  • Antibiotic & hydrocortisone ointment
  • CPR breathing barrier
  • Instant cold compress
  • Tweezers, thermometer, emergency blanket
  • Baby aspirin & ibuprofen
This first-aid guide is for educational reference only. It is not a substitute for formal first-aid training or professional medical advice. Always call emergency services (911) for life-threatening emergencies.
